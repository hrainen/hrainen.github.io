Voxel Engine | The First Chunk

I've been sick this week, but fortunately I had my computer on me and found the energy start a small project. Inspired by the trailer for the upcoming game Hytale, I set out to research how to make voxels like in Minecraft-esque games. Unfortunately, there are not too many resources available on this topic save for a few video tutorials and a couple blog posts.

However, I found enough information to get started and with fond memories of Minecraft in my head, I managed to get one chunk to render on screen in Unity.


Also, when you look at Unity's overdraw view of the scene, you can see a lack of highlighted cubes in the middle of the chunk ( save for places where cubes are missing ).


In other words, while I am using rows of cubes to build the chunk, as you can see in both pictures where a single cube is missing in various places, I do not render the cubes inside the chunk.

Currently, the way I accomplished this is to check every face of every cube in the chunk and look at the neighbor was visible, returning true for any face on the outer faces of the chunk.

In the future I'd like to be more efficient with rendering a chunk. I've been told to look into greedy meshing to save on vertices and triangles, but I have found even less information than I expected. Also, I need to think more about how I want to render multiple chunks. At some point I will need the engine to be able to render chunks as the player moves in a given direction and delete them when moving in another.
